ORGANIZATIONS

PHILIPPINES

Waves for Water

01

“Do what you love and help along the way.”

Jon Rose, the founder of Waves for Water(W4W), believed there could be a more meaningful way to participate in the world. He created W4W, a targeted, special-ops style, non-profit organization where they focus on correcting the imbalances of water scarcity, in developing communities around the world. Over the past ten years the organization have implemented 155 clean water programs, in 48 countries–using water filtration systems (150,000), bore-hole wells, and rainwater harvesting systems; impacting an estimated 3,750,000 people. W4W have responded to 33 major natural disasters, to date–including initiatives in Nepal, Bosnia, Philippines, Indonesia, Haiti, Japan, Chile, and Pakistan.

Planet Water Foundation

02

To transform the health and well-being of people in the world’s most impoverished communities through providing access to safe drinking water, sanitation, and hygiene education. Planet Water Foundation, founded in 2009 as a U.S. registered non-profit organization, is dedicated to providing safe drinking water to impoverished communities. By installing community-based water filtration systems, handwashing infrastructures, and implementing Water-Health & Hygiene Education programs, the organization focuses on two key areas: supporting schools, children, and local communities in the Asia-Pacific and Latin America regions, and providing safe drinking water in the wake of natural disasters globally.

Manila Water Foundation

03

Manila Water Foundation (MWF) serves as the social development arm of the Manila Water enterprise. Founded in 2005 as part of the company's sustainability efforts, the Foundation extends its water and sanitation expertise to marginalized communities beyond its concession areas. After a decade of service, MWF evolved into an independent non-profit organization dedicated to promoting water access, sanitation, and hygiene education (WASH) for communities in the Philippines lacking clean water and proper sanitation facilities.

OTHER COUNTRIES

Pacific Institute

01

The Pacific Institute envisions a world where society, the economy, and the environment have the water they need to thrive both now and in the future. As a global water think tank, the Pacific Institute combines science-based leadership with active outreach to influence sustainable water policies at local, national, and international levels. Since 1987, it has partnered with various stakeholders, from Fortune 500 companies to marginalized communities, to address the world’s most urgent water challenges. The institute not only identifies and recommends sensible water policies but also actively persuades decision-makers to adopt these solutions, promoting effective policies through research and analysis at all levels, from Congress to international forums.

World Water Council

02

The World Water Council is an international multi stakeholder platform organization whose mission is to mobilize action on critical water issues at all levels, including the highest decision-making level, by engaging people in debate and challenging conventional thinking. The Council focuses on the political dimensions of water security, adaptation and sustainability.

charity:water

03

charity: water is a nonprofit organization dedicated to providing clean and safe water to people worldwide. Since its founding in 2006, charity: water has pursued the ambitious goal of ending the global water crisis. Despite the enormity of the challenge, the organization remains optimistic, confident in its ability to solve the problem. With the support of local partners and generous donors, charity: water makes daily progress toward its mission. The organization believes that through collective effort, everyone will have access to clean water within our lifetime.

Generosity.org

04

Generosity is a nonprofit organization committed to transforming lives in African communities by providing access to clean water, essential resources, and greater opportunities for individual and community development. The organization firmly believes that clean water is a basic human right, yet millions in African communities still lack access to this vital resource. Through its dedicated efforts, Generosity has completed over 850 water projects, delivering clean and safe water to those most in need. These projects alleviate the burden of water scarcity, help prevent waterborne diseases, improve sanitation, and create opportunities for better health and economic growth.
